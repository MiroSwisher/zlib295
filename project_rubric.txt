This criterion is linked to a Learning Outcome Evaluation Methodology [10 pts]
Describe how you will evaluate your approach. The methodology should describe the following:


Describe any metrics you will measure to evaluate the efficacy or your tool. Describe how you will detect that a bug has occurred. Describe the basic evaluation you will perform to validate that your approach works. This basic evaluation may involve hand-crafted test applications with known bugs. If you devised any optimizations, describe any ablation studies you will perform to quantify the impact of your optimizations. Note: an ablation study is a study where you isolate the effect of a particular optimization. Describe the production/complex application you will evaluate your approach on. This application should not be a small toy program.

This criterion is linked to a Learning Outcome Evaluation on Production Application
Describe how your system performed on the production application. Were you able to identify any interesting behaviors? What were they? If you weren’t able to find anything interesting, that’s okay – you will not be graded on your ability to find bugs in software in-the-wild. Regardless of the outcome, investigate how well your tool was able to exercise the program. Did it stall out without exercising most of the program, or did it actually manage to explore most program paths? If the tool did not perform well, why do you think it couldn’t explore the program behavior more effectively?
